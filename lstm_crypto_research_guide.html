r/algotrading consensus on LSTM for crypto
"What actually works"
Feature engineering beats model complexity every time. Adding RSI, ATR, and volume profile to a simple LSTM outperforms a deep BiLSTM on raw OHLCV.
"Sequence length"
Most consensus: 30–60 candles for 15-min data. Longer (120+) often hurts because distant past is noisy, not signal. Your 60-candle choice is correct.
"Walk-Forward is non-negotiable"
Standard K-fold on time series = data leakage. Purged Walk-Forward (exactly what you have) is the community-recommended method from Lopez de Prado.
"Common traps"
Normalizing across the whole dataset (use rolling/online normalization). Not detrending. Predicting price instead of log-returns or binary direction.
"70-75% is realistic ceiling"
Community consensus: 70-75% directional accuracy on 15-min BTC is achievable with good features. 80%+ is suspicious — likely look-ahead bias. Anything below 55% is chance.

Your 70-75% accuracy roadmap — priority order
1
Add RSI, MACD, ATR, Bollinger %B to features.py
These 4 are the highest-impact additions. Papers show 5-8% accuracy jump from OHLCV-only. Use the ta library already in your requirements.txt.
2
Change pre-training target to binary log-return direction
Pre-train BiLSTM on binary up/down (not regression). Use Focal Loss (γ=2) to handle Hold class imbalance. This is the single biggest lever for classification accuracy.
3
Add self-attention layer after BiLSTM
Insert a scaled dot-product attention module between BiLSTM output and the dense head. Multiple papers show +2-4% accuracy improvement at negligible compute cost. PyTorch: nn.MultiheadAttention.
4
Scale up hidden units: 128 → 64 (two stacked layers)
Your current 64-dim output is fine, but add a second Bi-LSTM layer at 128 units before it. Add recurrent dropout (0.2) to both. This is the standard "sweet spot" from hyperparameter studies.
5
Add funding rate + open interest delta
Binance perpetual futures funding rate and OI changes are uniquely predictive for crypto (not available in stock data). Fetch via CCXT. Particularly useful for catching trend reversals.
6
Use Boruta or LGBM for feature selection
The top paper (82.44% accuracy) used Boruta to select on-chain features. Run LGBM feature importance on your full feature set and drop the bottom 30%. Prevents curse of dimensionality in LSTM input.
7
Rolling z-score normalization (not global)
Normalize features using a rolling 252-candle window, not global min-max. Global normalization introduces look-ahead bias (future price range leaks into past features). This is the most common silent bug in crypto LSTM projects.
